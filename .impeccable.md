# .impeccable.md — validator.com

Design context for the 10x redesign. Load-bearing for every impeccable skill invocation on this project. Written 2026-04-18 via `/impeccable teach`.

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: Crypto-native Solana holders. They already own SOL and actively shop validators on APY, commission, reliability, Jito MEV participation, and reputation. They read stakewiz.com and compare spec-sheets. They are not confused about what staking is — they are deciding which validator to delegate to and why.

**Context of use**: Desktop primarily. Often running wallets (Phantom, Solflare, Backpack) in adjacent tabs. Comparing validator.com side-by-side with peers. Decision window measured in minutes, not seconds. They will scroll, read, and judge on signal density and taste.

**Job to be done**: Select a validator they trust enough to delegate stake to, learn why validator.com is different, and execute the delegation with confidence. Secondary: return for tutorials, updates, and blog.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: **Trustworthy + expert + direct.**

Reads like a technical founder who respects your time. No fluff, no marketing rot, no rug-pull optimism. Every claim is grounded — uptime, commission, team credentials, operational transparency — and every sentence earns its place. The voice is the opposite of a DeFi Discord announcement. It's closer to a Stripe press page or a Paradigm research post.

**Emotional goals**: confidence that this team knows what they're doing; the calm of dealing with a professional operation; the subtle pride of choosing something with taste.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Direction**: **Editorial.** Typography-forward, generous whitespace, intentional asymmetry, big typographic moments. Degular does the heavy lifting. The page breathes. Numbers (APY, stake, commission) are treated with the gravity of a feature headline, not dashboard decoration.

**Theme**: Light, committed. No dark-mode toggle in Phase 1–4. Navy-tinted off-whites (OKLCH, `oklch(99% 0.003 260)` territory) for backgrounds. Warm off-black text (`oklch(18% 0.005 260)` territory). Brand accents (navy / cyan / orange) remain rare and load-bearing — 10% of visual weight, never more.

**References (what to learn from)**:
- **Stripe** (stripe.com, stripe.com/press) — editorial typography, technical confidence without shouting, generous whitespace, type hierarchy that carries the brand without illustration crutches
- **Jito** (jito.network) — closest direct crypto peer. Proof that validator/staking-adjacent sites can be editorial without losing crypto-native credibility

**Anti-references (what to explicitly reject)**:
- **Corporate fintech** (BNY Mellon, Fidelity, typical RIA websites) — trust-but-sterile. Stock photography, bulletproof copy, zero personality. Signals "I am insured" at the cost of any soul. We are NOT this.
- **Generic crypto DeFi** (cyan-on-black + purple-to-blue gradients + glassmorphism) — the default AI-crypto aesthetic. Neon accents, gradient text, glowing borders. Immediately signals "launched yesterday, pivoting tomorrow." We are NOT this.

Also explicitly banned (applies to every impeccable command from here on):
- v0 / shadcn default look: lucide icon above every heading, cards-wrapping-everything, identical padding, identical card grids
- Pure `#000` or `#fff` — all neutrals tinted toward `--brand-navy` hue
- `border-left: 4px solid [accent]` as a decorative accent stripe (impeccable BAN 1)
- Gradient text via `background-clip: text` (impeccable BAN 2)
- Monospace as "this is technical" shorthand — use it only when the content is actually code, hashes, or addresses
- Inter, Roboto, DM Sans, IBM Plex, Space Grotesk — and the entire impeccable reflex_fonts_to_reject list

### Motion

**Appetite**: **Expressive.** Scroll-driven layouts are on the table — GSAP ScrollTrigger (already in skill library via `gsap-scrolltrigger`), pinned sections, typographic reveals, data-entry animations for APY / active stake. Hero load animation staggers reveal.

Standard applies: every motion earns its existence. Nothing moves because motion is available. All respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Exponential easing only (ease-out-quart / quint / expo). No bounce, no elastic. Layout properties (width/height/padding/margin) never animated — transform + opacity only.

Phase 5 is where this gets built. Until then, treat motion budget as zero.

### Design Principles

These bind every later decision. When two options conflict, return here and pick the one that honors the principles.

1. **Typography is the hero.** Degular carries hierarchy. Paragraph rhythm and type-size contrast do the work that gradient text, glow borders, and decorative icons do in weaker designs. Pair Degular with a complementary body / mono face to be chosen in Phase 3 (do NOT default to Inter / Plex / DM Sans).

2. **Whitespace is a feature, not absence.** Editorial layouts earn their calm through generosity. Sections feel spacious; pages read vertically with pace. A crowded hero is a failure — we'd rather ship fewer words with more air.

3. **Data wears a suit, not a costume.** APY, stake, commission, uptime appear at feature-headline size in off-black on tinted off-white. No sparklines as decoration. No gradient numerals. The authority comes from the type treatment, not from chart chrome.

4. **Motion serves meaning.** Expressive is the appetite, but every transition answers "what changed, and why am I telling you?" Scroll reveals echo the editorial layout; hero metrics count up from 0 when they enter view; that is the budget. Everything else is state transitions only.

5. **Tint everything toward navy.** Backgrounds, surfaces, borders, text, shadows — all subtly shifted toward `--brand-navy`'s hue (OKLCH `~260°`). Pure black and pure white never appear. Orange and cyan remain the rare 10%: used for a CTA, a single accent rule, a moment of pop. Never as baseline.

## What this means operationally

- **Phase 3 foundation rewrite** of `app/globals.css` applies principles 5, 3, 2, 1 in that order. Colors → type scale → spacing → component primitives.
- **Phase 4 per-page passes** use `/impeccable:critique` to find where the page violates principles, then `/impeccable:typeset`, `/impeccable:layout`, `/impeccable:colorize`, `/impeccable:harden`, `/impeccable:polish` in sequence.
- **Phase 5 motion** uses `gsap-scrolltrigger` for the 2-3 earned moments (hero load, metric count-up, a mid-page pin if warranted). Nothing more.
- **`/impeccable:overdrive` is banned** for this project. Expressive motion ≠ WebGL spectacle. Trust-signaling does not welcome overdrive.
- **`/impeccable:bolder` is banned** unless a specific page is measurably timid. Our aesthetic is confident-quiet, not confident-loud.
- **`/impeccable:quieter` will be used heavily.** Inherited v0 code is the loud/crowded direction; we're pulling toward editorial restraint.
